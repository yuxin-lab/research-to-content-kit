# Medical Content Checklist

This checklist helps review medical, health, skincare, and biotechnology communication content before publication.

It is designed for science communication, patient education, expert content production, and public-facing medical education workflows.

## 1. Content type

Select the content type:

- Short video script
- Slide deck
- Xiaohongshu post
- Douyin video
- Article
- Product education content
- Expert interview
- Patient education material
- Research explanation

Select the topic category:

- Dermatology
- Acne

- Other

## 3. Audience

Who is the content for?

- General public
- Patients

- Clinicians
- Content creators

## 4. Scientific claim check

Review whether the content includes scientific claims.

### Claim type

- Mechanism explanation
- Research finding
- Clinical observation
- Expert opinion
- Product-related statement
- User feedback
- General education

### Questions to check

- Is the claim supported by a clear source?
- Is the claim overstated?
- Does the wording imply certainty when the evidence is limited?
- Does the content distinguish between research findings and product claims?
- Does the content confuse association with causation?
- Does the content imply diagnosis or treatment?

## 5. Risk wording checklist

Avoid or carefully review the following wording.

### High-risk wording

- Cure
- Treat
- Eliminate disease
- Guaranteed effect
- Works for everyone
- No side effects
- Medical-grade effect
- Replaces medication
- Clinically proven without evidence
- Direct disease treatment claim
- Drug-like mechanism in product promotion

### Medium-risk wording

- Repair
- Anti-inflammatory
- Sterilize
- Inhibit bacteria
- Activate cells
- Regenerate skin
- Reverse aging
- Medical technology
- Doctor-developed product
- Hospital-level effect

### Lower-risk alternatives

- May help support
- May be associated with
- Helps explain
- Supports daily care
- Designed for public education
- Based on research background
- Inspired by scientific research
- Used for non-medical daily care
- Individual results may vary
- Does not replace professional medical advice

## 6. Product-related content check

If the content mentions a product, check the following:

- Is the product category clear?
- Is the content avoiding drug-like claims?
- Is the content avoiding guaranteed results?
- Is the content avoiding disease treatment language?
- Is the content separating scientific background from product effect?
- Is the content using compliant, category-appropriate wording?
- Is the content avoiding statements that may mislead users?

## 7. Expert-related content check

If the content mentions a doctor, researcher, or expert, check the following:

- Does the content accurately describe the expert's role?
- Does the content avoid implying direct endorsement if not appropriate?
- Does the content avoid saying the expert personally guarantees results?
- Does the content distinguish between research background, clinical experience, and commercial product claims?
- Does the content use careful wording around collaboration, participation, or academic contribution?

## 8. Safer expression examples

### Instead of:

This product treats acne.

### Consider:

This content discusses acne-related skin care from a science communication perspective.

---

### Instead of:

This ingredient eliminates inflammation.

### Consider:

Research suggests this pathway may be related to inflammatory responses.

---

### Instead of:

Doctor-developed acne treatment.

### Consider:

Developed with reference to dermatology research and expert communication insights.

---

### Instead of:

Clinically proven to work.

### Consider:

Early feedback and structured observation may help guide further evaluation.

## 9. Final review

Before publishing, check:

- Is the content easy to understand?
- Is the scientific explanation clear?
- Are risky claims avoided?
- Are product claims separated from research background?
- Is the content suitable for the intended platform?
- Is the audience expectation managed?
- Is there a reminder that the content is for education only when needed?

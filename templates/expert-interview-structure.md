It is designed for researchers, clinicians, medical educators, science communicators, and content teams who need to turn long conversations into reusable content assets.

## 11. Risk wording review

Check whether the interview-based content includes:

- Diagnosis claims
- Treatment claims
- Guaranteed results
- Overstated expert endorsement
- Product claims without proper support
- Confusion between research background and product function
- Unclear distinction between personal opinion and evidence-based explanation

## 12. Safer wording examples

### Instead of:

The expert proves this product works.

### Consider:

The expert perspective helps explain the scientific background of this topic.

---

### Instead of:

This method treats acne.

### Consider:

This content explains acne-related mechanisms from a public education perspective.

---

### Instead of:

Doctor-developed product.

### Consider:

Developed with reference to expert communication insights and research background.

# Social Post Template

This template helps transform research, medical education, expert interviews, or science communication topics into structured social media posts.

It is designed for public education content on platforms such as Xiaohongshu, Douyin, WeChat, and other short-form or social platforms.

## 2. Public-facing question

Convert the topic into a question that the audience may care about.

Examples:

- Why does acne keep coming back?
- Does sugar really make acne worse?
- Does staying up late affect the skin barrier?
- What do most people misunderstand about this topic?
- What should users know before making a decision?

## 9. Risk wording review

Check whether the post includes:

- Diagnosis claims
- Treatment claims
- Guaranteed results
- Overstated scientific claims
- Disease-related product claims
- Drug-like mechanism claims in product promotion
- Misleading expert endorsement
- Confusion between research background and product function

## 10. Safer wording examples

### Instead of:

This product treats acne.

### Consider:

This content discusses acne-related skin care from a science communication perspective.

---

### Instead of:

This ingredient eliminates inflammation.

### Consider:

Research suggests this pathway may be related to inflammatory responses.

---

### Instead of:

Doctor-developed product.

### Consider:

Developed with reference to expert communication insights and research background.

---

### Instead of:

Clinically proven to work for everyone.

### Consider:

Individual situations vary, and further evaluation may be needed.

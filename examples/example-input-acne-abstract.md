# Example Input: Acne Research Abstract

This file provides an example input for testing the Research to Content Kit workflow.

The example is written as a simplified research-style input, not as a real paper abstract.

## Topic

Acne, inflammation, lifestyle factors, and skin barrier communication.

## Source type

Simplified research-style abstract for workflow testing.

## Target audience

General public, skincare users, students, and science communication teams.

## Example abstract

Acne is a common skin condition that may involve multiple biological and environmental factors, including sebum production, follicle blockage, inflammatory response, microbial environment, lifestyle, sleep, diet, stress, and skin barrier condition.

Public-facing acne education often focuses on visible skin symptoms or product use, but structured science communication may help explain acne as a multi-factor skin condition.

This example input is designed to test how research-style material can be transformed into:

- Short video scripts
- Slide outlines
- Social media posts
- Compliance-aware wording reviews
- Public education content structures

## Key concepts

- Sebum production
- Follicle blockage
- Inflammatory response
- Skin barrier
- Lifestyle factors
- Public education
- Safe wording
- Non-medical communication

## Communication goal

Transform this input into a structured public-facing explanation that helps users understand why acne may keep coming back.

## Risk notes

The output should avoid:

- Diagnosis claims
- Treatment claims
- Guaranteed improvement claims
- Disease cure language
- Product effect claims
- Overstated expert endorsement
- Drug-like mechanism claims in product promotion

## Suggested output formats

- Short video script
- Slide outline
- Social post draft
- Safer wording review

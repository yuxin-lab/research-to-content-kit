# Example: Acne Science Communication

This example shows how to transform a medical or dermatology topic into structured public-facing science communication content.

Topic: acne science communication  
Audience: general public, students, skincare users, medical content creators  
Format: short video script, slide outline, and social post structure

## 1. Source topic

Acne is a common skin condition related to multiple factors, including sebum production, follicle blockage, inflammatory response, microbial environment, lifestyle, stress, sleep, diet, and skin barrier status.

This example does not provide diagnosis or treatment advice. It is used only to demonstrate how a scientific topic can be converted into public education content.

## 2. Public-facing question

Why does acne keep coming back even after skincare routines change?

## 3. Core message

Acne is not only a surface-level skin problem. It may involve follicle blockage, oil production, inflammation, lifestyle factors, and long-term skin management.

## 4. Short video structure

### Opening hook

You may think acne is just a skincare problem, but the real reason can be more complicated.

### Part 1: Common misunderstanding

Many people blame one product, one food, or one night of staying up late.

But acne often involves several factors working together.

### Part 2: Simple science explanation

Acne may be related to:

- Excess sebum
- Follicle blockage
- Inflammatory response
- Skin barrier condition
- Lifestyle factors such as sleep, stress, and diet

### Part 3: Public education message

Instead of only asking “what product should I use,” it may be more useful to understand:

- Why the skin is reacting
- Whether the problem is repeated
- Whether the skin barrier is also affected
- Whether professional evaluation is needed

### Closing

For repeated or severe acne, public education content is not a substitute for professional medical advice.

## 5. Slide outline

### Slide 1: Why acne keeps coming back

Introduce the topic.

### Slide 2: Acne is not only a surface problem

Explain that acne may involve multiple biological and lifestyle factors.

### Slide 3: Follicle blockage

Use a simple diagram or animation to show blocked pores.

### Slide 4: Sebum and inflammatory response

Explain in simple words.

### Slide 5: Lifestyle factors

Mention sleep, stress, diet, and daily routine.

### Slide 6: Skin barrier

Explain why over-cleaning or irritation may make skin management harder.

### Slide 7: What public education can do

Help users understand mechanisms and avoid common mistakes.

### Slide 8: What public education cannot do

It cannot replace diagnosis, prescription treatment, or professional medical advice.

## 6. Social post structure

### Title

Acne keeps coming back? It may not be just a skincare problem.

### Subtitle

A simple science communication example for understanding repeated acne.

### Main points

- Acne may involve more than one cause.
- Oil, blockage, inflammation, and skin barrier all matter.
- Lifestyle factors may also influence skin condition.
- Repeated or severe acne should be evaluated by professionals.

### Closing reminder

This content is for science education only and does not replace professional medical advice.

## 7. Wording review

### Avoid

- This method cures acne.
- This product treats acne.
- This ingredient eliminates inflammation.
- Everyone can improve quickly.

### Safer wording

- Acne may be related to multiple factors.
- This content explains possible mechanisms.
- Individual situations vary.
- Professional evaluation may be needed for repeated or severe acne.

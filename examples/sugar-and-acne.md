# Example: Sugar and Acne

This example shows how to transform a research-related dermatology topic into public-facing science communication content.

Topic: sugar, high-glycemic diet, and acne  
Audience: general public, students, skincare users, medical content creators  
Format: short video script and social post structure

## 1. Source topic

Some studies suggest that high-glycemic diets may be associated with acne in certain populations.

This example is for science communication only. It does not provide medical diagnosis, treatment advice, or dietary prescription.

## 2. Public-facing question

Can eating too much sugar make acne worse?

## 3. Core message

Sugar does not directly “create acne” in everyone, but high-glycemic eating patterns may be related to acne through insulin, hormone-related pathways, sebum production, and inflammatory responses.

## 4. Short video structure

### Opening hook

If you keep breaking out after drinking sweet drinks or eating desserts, the problem may not be only your skincare routine.

### Part 1: Common misunderstanding

Many people think acne is caused by dirt, poor cleansing, or using the wrong skincare product.

But acne can also be related to internal and lifestyle factors.

### Part 2: Simple science explanation

High-glycemic foods may cause blood sugar to rise quickly.

This may influence insulin-related pathways, which may further affect:

- Sebum production
- Follicle environment
- Inflammatory response
- Acne-related skin condition

### Part 3: What this does not mean

This does not mean everyone must completely avoid sugar.

It also does not mean one dessert will definitely cause acne.

The key message is that repeated high-sugar or high-glycemic eating patterns may be worth paying attention to, especially for people who already have acne-prone skin.

### Closing

For repeated or severe acne, diet is only one possible factor. Professional evaluation may still be needed.

## 5. Social post structure

### Title

Does sugar really make acne worse?

### Subtitle

A simple science explanation for acne-prone skin.

### Main points

- Acne is related to multiple factors.
- High-glycemic diets may be associated with acne in some people.
- Sugar may influence acne-related pathways indirectly.
- One food does not explain every acne problem.
- Repeated or severe acne should not rely only on skincare changes.

### Closing reminder

This content is for science education only and does not replace professional medical advice.

## 6. Visual suggestions

Possible visuals:

- Sweet drinks
- Desserts
- Blood sugar curve illustration
- Skin oil production animation
- Acne mechanism diagram
- Doctor or expert explaining
- Slide-style keyword cards

## 7. Wording review

### Avoid

- Sugar causes acne in everyone.
- Stop sugar and acne will disappear.
- This method treats acne.
- This diet cures acne.

### Safer wording

- High-glycemic diets may be associated with acne in some people.
- Sugar may influence acne-related pathways indirectly.
- Acne is usually related to multiple factors.
- Individual skin conditions vary.
- This content is for public education only.

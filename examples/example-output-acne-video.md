# Example Output: Acne Short Video Script

This file shows an example output generated from `example-input-acne-abstract.md`.

It demonstrates how a research-style input can be transformed into a structured public-facing short video script.

## Topic

Why does acne keep coming back?

## Target audience

General public, skincare users, students, and science communication teams.

## Content type

Short video script.

## Core message

Acne is not only a surface-level skin problem. It may involve sebum production, follicle blockage, inflammatory response, lifestyle factors, and skin barrier condition.

## Public-facing question

Why does acne keep coming back even after changing skincare products?

## Short video title

Acne keeps coming back? It may not be just your skincare routine.

## Opening hook

If you keep breaking out again and again, the problem may not be only one skincare product.

## Full script

Many people think acne is caused by poor cleansing, eating one specific food, or using the wrong skincare product.

But acne is usually more complicated.

From a science communication perspective, acne may be related to several factors at the same time.

First, the skin may produce too much sebum.

Second, the follicle opening may become blocked.

Third, inflammatory responses may be involved.

And in daily life, sleep, stress, diet, and skin barrier condition may also affect acne-prone skin.

So if acne keeps coming back, it may be more useful to ask:

Why is my skin repeatedly reacting?

Is the skin barrier also under stress?

Are lifestyle factors making the condition harder to manage?

And do I need professional evaluation?

This content is for public education only. Repeated or severe acne should not rely only on skincare changes.

## Visual suggestions

- Close-up of acne-prone skin concept image
- Simple follicle blockage diagram
- Sebum and inflammation keyword card
- Lifestyle scenes: staying up late, stress, sweet drinks
- Skin barrier concept diagram
- Final summary card

## Risk wording review

### Avoided wording

- Cure acne
- Treat acne
- Guaranteed improvement
- Works for everyone
- Product effect claim
- Drug-like mechanism claim

### Safer wording used

- May be related to
- From a science communication perspective
- May also affect
- Individual conditions vary
- Professional evaluation may be needed
- Public education only
